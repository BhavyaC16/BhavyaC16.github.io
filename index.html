Hello! I am Bhavya Chopra, a pre-doctoral Research Fellow with the PROSE Team at Microsoft, advised by Dr. Austin Z. Henley. I completed my bachelors in Computer Science and Design engineering (with honors) from IIIT-Delhi in 2022.

My research interests lie at the intersection of Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), Software Engineering, designing Intelligent Systems, and Accessibility Design. I am interested in exploring and conducting mixed-methods research to design systems for end-users and developers. In particular, my recent work, advised by Dr. Austin Henley, Dr. Chris Parnin, Dr. Anna Fariha, and Dr. Ashish Tiwari, focuses on uncovering challenges in conversational and recommendation based AI systems for data science tasks. Currently, I am involved in leveraging findings to design productive human-AI experiences for Excel.

Prior to my research fellowship, I worked with Prof. Richa Gupta to design inclusive and frugal storytelling interfaces for children with blindness and visual impairment. I pursued my bachelor thesis under the guidance of Prof. Rahul Purandare, where I leveraged program analysis techniques to understand how developers make use of assertions in Java open-source repositories.
