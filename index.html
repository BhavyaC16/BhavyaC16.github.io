Hello! I am Bhavya Chopra, a pre-doctoral Research Fellow with the PROSE Team at Microsoft, advised by Dr. Austin Z. Henley. I completed my bachelors in Computer Science and Design engineering (with honors) from IIIT-Delhi in 2022.

My research interests lie in Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), Human-AI Interaction, and Software Engineering. I am interested in exploring and conducting mixed-methods research to design systems for end-users and developers. My recent research, advised by Dr. Austin Z. Henley, Dr. Chris Parnin, Dr. Anna Fariha, and Dr. Ashish Tiwari, focuses on uncovering challenges in conversational and recommendation based AI systems for data science tasks. Currently, I am leveraging our research findings to design productive human-AI experiences for Excel.

Prior to my current role, I worked in accessibility design with Prof. Richa Gupta to design an inclusive and frugal storytelling interface for children with blindness and visual impairment. I pursued my bachelor thesis under the guidance of Prof. Rahul Purandare, where I used program analysis techniques to understand how developers make use of assertions in Java open-source repositories.
